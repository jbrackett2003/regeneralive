## ⭐ STEP 1 — Sign up for affiliate networks (do these first)

These are the "rails" that most brands ride on. Once you have accounts, applying to individual brands is just a click.

### 1. Impact.com 🥇 *(highest priority)*
- **Sign up:** https://app.impact.com/campaign-promo-signup/sign-up.brand
- **Why first:** ButcherBox, Patagonia (incl. Provisions), Crowd Cow, and Grandy Organics are ALL on Impact. One profile, four brand applications.
- **Approval:** ~24–48 hours for the network account.
- **Required:** Your live site URL (`www.regeneralive.com`), tax info (W-9 if US), basic traffic numbers.

### 2. ShareASale (now part of Awin) 🥈
- **Sign up:** https://account.shareasale.com/newaffiliatesignup.cfm
- **Why next:** Hosts a long tail of supplement and natural-food brands. Even if a brand isn't there now, you'll want this account for future expansion.
- **Approval:** ~1–3 business days.
- **Required:** Same as Impact.

### 3. FlexOffers (aggregator) 🥉
- **Sign up:** https://www.flexoffers.com/affiliate-application
- **Why:** FlexOffers is a "network of networks" — it gives you backup access to Ancient Nutrition, Brodo, Bonafide, Gaia Herbs, etc. without applying separately. Lower commission rates than going direct, but instant approval for many brands.
- **Approval:** Often instant for the network; per-brand approval still required.

> **Skip if pressed for time:** Skimlinks and Sovrn Commerce are also options but lower priority — they take a small cut of every sale, so you earn less per click than going direct.

## 🛒 BACKUP — Amazon Associates (covers everything)

If a brand has zero affiliate program, **Amazon Associates** is the catch-all. Most of these brands sell on Amazon, so an Amazon affiliate link captures the click.

- **Sign up:** https://affiliate-program.amazon.com/
- **Commission:** 1–4% on grocery (low — but better than $0)
- **Use case:** Lundberg, Frontier Co-op, Maple Hill, anything else without a direct program.

> Amazon's commissions are low, but the **conversion rate is incredibly high** because most readers already have an Amazon account. Worth having as a fallback.

## ⚠️ One important note about FTC compliance

When your site links to affiliate URLs, the FTC requires a clear disclosure. You already have one at `/disclosure` — but make sure to also:

- Mention "affiliate-supported" in the footer (already done)
- Add `rel="sponsored nofollow"` to affiliate links in editorial content
- For Instagram/email content: include `#affiliate` or `#ad` when linking

Your site is already configured for compliance — just be careful when you do social posting.
